summary: Digital knowledge sharing platforms connect educators, researchers, and staff across universities, enabling seamless collaboration, resource exchange, and professional learning in real time or asynchronously.
description: Digital knowledge sharing platforms—like Moodle, Teams, Slack, and LinkedIn—enable universities to centralise communication, share resources, and coordinate learning, teaching, or research activities. Whether hosting discussions, storing materials, or collaborating on projects, these tools break down silos and improve efficiency, accessibility, and knowledge continuity across staff, departments, and global partnerships.
outcomes: |
  Improved collaboration between faculty and admin.
  Faster feedback loops and shared decision-making.
  Better access to institutional knowledge and digital assets.
  Enhanced engagement in cross-disciplinary or Erasmus+ projects.
instructions: |
  Set up accounts for staff and define team spaces (e.g., by faculty, theme, or project).
  Upload shared resources (docs, templates, guides).
  Use discussion threads or chat channels for topic-based collaboration.
  Schedule knowledge-sharing sessions or regular updates.
