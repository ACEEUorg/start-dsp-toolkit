summary: The Role Navigator helps non-business academics identify their potential contributions to entrepreneurship education. It increases institutional awareness and supports inclusive participation across disciplines through tailored role suggestions and personalized feedback.
description: The Role Navigator is designed for non-business academics seeking to understand their role in entrepreneurship education. Through a short assessment, participants receive personalized feedback highlighting how their teaching style, interests, and disciplinary strengths can contribute to entrepreneurial learning. The tool encourages wider academic participation beyond traditional business faculties.
outcomes: |
  Broader engagement of non-business academics in entrepreneurship education
  Increased interdisciplinary collaboration within universities
  Enhanced role clarity and motivation among academic staff
  Identification of faculty with high potential for entrepreneurial initiatives
  Inclusion of diverse perspectives in entrepreneurship teaching and support
instructions: |
  Share the assessment link with faculty through newsletters, training sessions, or onboarding materials.
  Invite non-business academics to complete the short self-assessment (approx. 10–15 minutes).
  Participants reflect on their discipline, interests, and current teaching methods.
  Upon submission, each participant receives a personalized PDF feedback report.
  Encourage participants to review suggested roles and consider concrete next steps (e.g., mentoring, co-teaching).
  Optionally, host follow-up sessions to connect academics and discuss collaboration opportunities.
