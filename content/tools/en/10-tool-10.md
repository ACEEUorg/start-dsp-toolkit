instructions: |
  This is a template that universities or organisations can adapt and remake to suit their own context. The steps below guide users through collecting and using financial data to inform strategic planning.
  1. Distribute the Survey
  Share the Google Form survey link with key representatives from faculties such as Business, Engineering, Social Sciences, Science, and departments including Finance and Entrepreneurship Centres.
  2. Request Qualitative and Quantitative Input
  Ask each respondent to provide both financial data (e.g. budgets, spending figures) and qualitative insights on how entrepreneurship-related funds are used.
  3. Gather and Consolidate Responses
  Use Google Forms to automatically collect input. Export the responses for further analysis or review key trends using built-in reporting features.
  4. Generate Initial Findings
  Identify overall investment levels, primary funding sources, and how funds are allocated across departments. Note any missing data or unclear spending.
  5. Identify Strategic Opportunities
  Spot underfunded areas (e.g. incubators or student startup support) and compare departmental investment levels against overall entrepreneurship education goals.
  6. Report and Recommend Actions
  Present your findings to leadership. Use the insights to suggest improvements in resource distribution or to embed regular financial tracking into university planning processes.
